About the Process

Photolithography is a fundamental technique used in nano fabrication and semiconductor manufacturing. It transfers geometric patterns from a mask onto a silicon wafer using light-sensitive photoresist material.


The process includes coating, mask alignment, UV exposure, development, and etching. These steps create precise microscopic structures required for integrated circuits, sensors, processors, and memory devices.


High precision, repeatability, and scalability make photolithography one of the most important fabrication techniques in modern electronics.
